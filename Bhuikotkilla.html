Bhuikot Killa, Ahilyanagar

Location: Near Ahilyanagar, Maharashtra

Bhuikot Killa, also known as Ahmednagar Fort, is a prominent land fort located in the heart of Ahmednagar city in Maharashtra. Unlike hill forts, Bhuikot Killa is built on flat land and is surrounded by strong defensive structures.

The fort was constructed in the 15th century by Malik Ahmad Nizam Shah, the founder of the Nizam Shahi dynasty. It served as the administrative and military headquarters of the Ahmednagar Sultanate.

Bhuikot Killa is known for its massive circular fortification walls, deep moat, and strategically designed bastions. The fort’s architecture reflects a blend of Islamic military engineering and Deccan construction techniques.

The fort holds great historical importance due to its association with Chand Bibi, the brave queen of Ahmednagar, who successfully defended the fort against the Mughal forces led by Emperor Akbar in the late 16th century. Her leadership and courage are remembered as a symbol of resistance.

During later years, the fort came under the control of the Mughals, Marathas, and the British. Under British rule, Bhuikot Killa was used as a prison, most notably to detain leaders of the Indian freedom movement, including Jawaharlal Nehru.

Inside the fort complex, visitors can find remains of palaces, mosques, barracks, wells, and cannons, along with structures that once housed administrative offices and royal quarters.

Today, Bhuikot Killa stands as a significant historical monument and a protected site. It attracts history enthusiasts, tourists, and students, serving as a reminder of Ahmednagar’s rich political, military, and cultural heritage.
