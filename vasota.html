Vasota Fort

Location: Near Bamnoli, Maharashtra

Vasota Fort, also known as Vyaghrapada, is a magnificent fort located in the Satara district of Maharashtra, deep within the dense forests of the Koyna Wildlife Sanctuary. Perched at an altitude of approximately 3,842 feet (1,171 meters), this fort is surrounded by the lush greenery of the Sahyadri mountain range and offers breathtaking views of the Koyna backwaters. Due to its remote and rugged terrain, Vasota is one of the most adventurous forts in Maharashtra.

Vasota Fort was originally built by the Shilahara dynasty in the 11th century. However, it gained prominence under the rule of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, who captured it in 1655 and strengthened its fortifications. The fort served as a key military base for the Marathas, providing a strategic advantage due to its natural defenses and difficult accessibility.

In 1818, during the Third Anglo-Maratha War, the British forces, under General John Malcolm, captured Vasota. Fearing its use by the Marathas for guerrilla warfare, the British destroyed a significant portion of the fort. Today, visitors can still find remnants of its past glory, including its massive walls, ruined structures, and ancient temples.

One of the most significant structures within the fort is the Nageshwar Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is a popular pilgrimage site and adds to the spiritual aura of the place. The fort also has several water cisterns that provided a continuous water supply to its inhabitants.

Vasota Fort is accessible only by a combination of boat ride and trekking. Trekkers must take a boat from Bamnoli village across the Shiv Sagar Lake and then undertake a thrilling jungle trek of around 3-4 hours. The dense forest, rich with wildlife including leopards, deer, and various bird species, makes this trek an unforgettable experience.

Today, Vasota is a paradise for trekkers and nature lovers. It is a perfect blend of history, adventure, and scenic beauty. The fort remains a testimony to the Maratha resilience and the natural strength of their forts. The challenging trek and breathtaking views make Vasota an unforgettable destination for those seeking an offbeat historical adventure.
