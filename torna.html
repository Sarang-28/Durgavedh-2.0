Torna Fort

Location: Near Pune, Maharashtra

Torna Fort, also known as Prachandgad, is one of the most significant forts in the history of the Maratha Empire. It is located about 50 km from Pune and stands at an impressive height of approximately 4,603 feet (1,403 meters), making it the highest fort in Pune district. The fort played a crucial role in the early conquests of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj and became a symbol of Maratha pride and resistance.

The fort has a rich historical background and is believed to have been constructed in the 13th century during the rule of the Shilahara dynasty. However, it gained prominence when Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj captured it in 1646 at the young age of 16. This was his first major conquest, marking the beginning of the Maratha Swarajya. After taking control of the fort, he expanded its fortifications and renamed it Prachandgad, meaning "massive fort."

One of the most notable structures within the fort is the Bini Darwaza, the main entrance, along with Kothi Darwaza and Konkan Darwaza. The fort also houses historical remnants like the Mahal, believed to be the residence of the fort’s administrators, and Goddess Mengai Temple, where Shivaji Maharaj is said to have prayed. The fort has multiple water reservoirs and storage facilities, which made it a self-sustaining stronghold.

In 1665, following the Treaty of Purandar, the fort was handed over to the Mughals. However, it was soon recaptured by the Marathas in 1670 under the leadership of Shivaji Maharaj, reaffirming their dominance in the region. After Shivaji Maharaj’s reign, the fort changed hands multiple times, with the Mughals, Peshwas, and British all controlling it at different points in history.

Torna Fort is famous for its breathtaking views of the Sahyadri mountain range and is a favorite trekking destination for adventure enthusiasts. The trek to the top is considered challenging due to steep climbs and rough terrain, but it rewards visitors with stunning landscapes and a glimpse into Maratha history.

The fort stands as a powerful symbol of Maratha bravery and the vision of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, reminding people of his determination to establish an independent kingdom. Today, it is a popular historical and trekking site, attracting history lovers, nature enthusiasts, and adventure seekers alike.
