Pawangad Fort, Kolhapur

Location: Near Kolhapur, Maharashtra

Pawangad Fort is a historically important hill fort located in the Kolhapur district of Maharashtra. It is situated in the Sahyadri mountain range at an altitude of approximately 980 meters above sea level and lies close to the famous Panhala Fort, forming an important defensive pair.

Pawangad Fort was constructed during the Adil Shahi period and functioned as a subsidiary fort to Panhala. Its primary role was to support Panhala Fort by monitoring enemy movements and strengthening the defensive network of the region.

The fort gained importance during the reign of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, when it came under Maratha control. Pawangad was used as a military outpost and lookout point, helping in the protection of the surrounding areas and trade routes.

Although smaller in size compared to major forts, Pawangad Fort was strategically valuable due to its elevated position and proximity to Panhala. Together, the two forts created a strong defensive system that made enemy advances extremely difficult.

The fort structure includes remains of stone ramparts, bastions, gateways, water tanks, and ruins of old buildings. These features indicate that the fort was capable of housing a small garrison and sustaining itself during short sieges.

Over time, Pawangad Fort witnessed changes in control between the Marathas and the British. With the decline of fort-based warfare, it gradually lost its military significance.

Today, Pawangad Fort stands in a partially ruined condition and is visited by trekkers, history enthusiasts, and nature lovers. The fort offers panoramic views of Panhala, surrounding valleys, and the Western Ghats, especially during the monsoon season.
