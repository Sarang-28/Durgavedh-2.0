Bahadurgad, Ahilyanagar

Location: Near Ahilyanagar, Maharashtra

Bahadurgad Fort is a historic land fort located in the Ahmednagar district of Maharashtra. It lies near the village of Pedgaon on the banks of the Bhima River and is also popularly known as Pedgaon Fort.

The fort was constructed in the 16th century during the reign of the Nizam Shahi dynasty. Due to its location on the plains, Bahadurgad was built as a strong military and administrative center rather than a hill fort.

Bahadurgad Fort is noted for its massive stone walls, circular bastions, and deep surrounding moat. The fort’s defensive architecture reflects advanced Deccan military engineering, designed to withstand artillery attacks.

The fort gained its present name during the Mughal period after being renamed in honor of Bahadur Khan, a Mughal commander. Over time, the fort came under the control of the Mughals, Marathas, and the British.

Inside the fort complex, visitors can find remains of palaces, mosques, residential quarters, water tanks, and granaries. These structures indicate that the fort once supported a sizable military garrison and administrative staff.

Bahadurgad Fort also holds cultural significance, as it houses the temple of Kanifnath Maharaj, which continues to attract devotees from nearby regions.

Today, Bahadurgad Fort stands in a partially ruined condition but remains an important historical landmark. It is visited by history enthusiasts, architecture students, and local tourists, serving as a reminder of Ahmednagar’s rich historical legacy.
