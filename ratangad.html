Ratangad , Ahilyanagar

Location: Near Ahilyanagar, Maharashtra

Ratangad Fort is an ancient hill fort located in the Ahmednagar district of Maharashtra. Situated in the Sahyadri mountain range near the village of Ratanwadi, the fort stands at an altitude of approximately 1,297 meters above sea level.

The fort is believed to be over 400 years old and gained prominence during the reign of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, who recognized its strategic importance in controlling the surrounding region.

Ratangad played a key role in guarding the ancient trade routes connecting the Konkan coast with the Deccan plateau. Due to its elevated position, the fort served as an effective watchtower over the Bhandardara region.

One of the most distinctive features of Ratangad Fort is the Nedhe (Eye of the Needle), a natural rock formation at the top of the fort that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys.

The fort complex includes remains of stone fortification walls, bastions, rock-cut water cisterns, and ancient gateways, indicating that it once housed a military garrison.

Ratangad Fort is closely associated with the nearby Amruteshwar Temple at Ratanwadi, a fine example of Hemadpanthi architecture dating back to the 9th century.

Today, Ratangad Fort is one of the most popular trekking destinations in Ahmednagar district. It attracts trekkers, photographers, history enthusiasts, and nature lovers, especially during the monsoon season when the region is lush and scenic.
