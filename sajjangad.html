Sajjangad Fort

Location: Near Satara, Maharashtra

Sajjangad Fort, meaning "Fort of Good People", is a historically and spiritually significant fort located in the Satara district of Maharashtra. Situated at an altitude of approximately 3,350 feet (1,020 meters), this fort is best known as the final resting place of the revered saint Samarth Ramdas Swami, the spiritual mentor of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. Unlike other forts in Maharashtra, Sajjangad is more of a spiritual site than a military fortress.

The fort was originally built by the Shilahara dynasty in the 12th century. Over the years, it was controlled by the Bahmani Sultanate and later the Adilshahi dynasty of Bijapur. In 1673, Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj captured Sajjangad and handed it over to Samarth Ramdas Swami to serve as a spiritual and social reform center. Since then, the fort has been considered a sacred place for followers of Hindu philosophy.

Unlike other Maratha forts, Sajjangad did not play a major role in military campaigns. Instead, it became a center of spirituality, social service, and education. Samarth Ramdas Swami, a strong proponent of Bhakti (devotion) and Karma (duty), spent his later years here, guiding Shivaji Maharaj and the people on good governance, self-discipline, and devotion. His teachings continue to influence many people across Maharashtra.

After the fall of the Maratha Empire, the fort came under British control in 1818 following the Third Anglo-Maratha War. However, it was never heavily damaged, and its spiritual significance remained intact. Today, the fort is managed by the Shri Ramdas Swami Sansthan, which ensures its maintenance and organizes daily prayers and food offerings (Mahaprasad) for visitors.

The main attractions at Sajjangad include the Samadhi (final resting place) of Samarth Ramdas Swami, the Ram Temple, and prayer halls. The fort offers panoramic views of the Urmodi Dam and the lush Sahyadri hills. Visitors must climb a series of well-maintained stone steps to reach the fort, making it an accessible yet fulfilling experience.

Today, Sajjangad stands as a beacon of spirituality and service. Devotees and tourists visit throughout the year, especially during Ram Navami and Dasara, to pay homage to Samarth Ramdas Swami and experience the serene, divine atmosphere. The fort remains a symbol of the Maratha legacy and the deep connection between governance and spirituality.
