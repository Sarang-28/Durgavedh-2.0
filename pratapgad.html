Pratapgad Fort

Location: Near Mahabaleshwar, Maharashtra

Pratapgad Fort is one of the most famous forts in Maharashtra, located in the Satara district, about 24 km from Mahabaleshwar. Perched at an elevation of approximately 3,543 feet (1,080 meters), this fort is historically significant due to the legendary Battle of Pratapgad fought between Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj and Afzal Khan of the Adilshahi Sultanate in 1659. The battle marked a turning point in the rise of the Maratha Empire.

The fort was built by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1656 under the guidance of his prime minister, Moropant Trimbak Pingle. It was constructed to strengthen Maratha control over the Nira and Koyna river valleys and to defend against invaders from the Adilshahi Sultanate. The fort is divided into two sections: the Upper Fort, located on a steep hilltop, and the Lower Fort, which extends along the hill’s southern and eastern slopes.

The most significant event in the fort’s history is the Battle of Pratapgad, which took place on November 10, 1659. The Adilshahi general Afzal Khan marched towards Pratapgad with a massive army to eliminate Shivaji Maharaj. However, Shivaji Maharaj, using his brilliant strategy, lured Afzal Khan into a private meeting where he used the concealed Bagh Nakh (tiger claws) to kill him in self-defense. Following this, the Marathas decisively defeated the Adilshahi forces, solidifying their dominance in the region.

Pratapgad Fort features several key attractions, including the famous Mahadev Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, and the Bhavani Temple, where Shivaji Maharaj is believed to have prayed before the battle. The fort also houses the Afzal Khan’s Tomb and the large statue of Shivaji Maharaj, which was installed in 1957 by the first Chief Minister of Maharashtra, Yashwantrao Chavan.

Today, Pratapgad Fort is a major tourist destination, attracting history enthusiasts and trekkers. The route to the fort is well-developed, and visitors can explore its grand gates, fortifications, secret passages, and scenic viewpoints. The fort remains a proud symbol of Maratha bravery and the strategic genius of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj.
