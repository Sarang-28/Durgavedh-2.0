Purandar Fort

Location: Near Pune, Maharashtra

Purandar Fort is one of the most significant forts in Maharashtra, located around 50 km from Pune in the Sahyadri mountain range. Standing at an elevation of approximately 4,472 feet (1,362 meters), the fort has played a crucial role in the history of the Maratha Empire. It is not only a symbol of Maratha pride but also the birthplace of Chhatrapati Sambhaji Maharaj, the son of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj.

The history of Purandar Fort dates back to ancient times and was ruled by various dynasties, including the Yadavas, Bahmani Sultanate, and the Adilshahi. However, the fort gained prominence when Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj took control of it in 1646 at the young age of 16. This marked the beginning of his military conquests and the foundation of the Maratha Empire.

In 1665, the fort was at the center of an intense battle between the Marathas and the Mughals under the command of Mirza Raja Jai Singh. After a fierce fight, Shivaji Maharaj was forced to sign the Treaty of Purandar, under which he had to surrender 23 forts, including Purandar, to the Mughals. However, in 1670, Shivaji Maharaj launched a successful campaign to reclaim the fort from Mughal control.

One of the most remarkable features of Purandar Fort is its two levels: the lower fort, called Machi, and the upper fort, known as Balekilla. The Balekilla houses the ruins of old palaces, temples, and military structures. There is also the famous Kedareshwar Temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, which is an important pilgrimage site.

The fort also has a significant connection to the Peshwa rulers, as it was the family stronghold of Bajirao I. During the British rule, the fort was used as a prison and a military training center, further adding to its historical importance.

Today, Purandar Fort is a popular trekking destination, offering breathtaking views of the Western Ghats. The fort's rugged terrain, historical monuments, and serene surroundings make it a favorite spot for history lovers and adventure seekers. The trek to the top is moderately challenging, with steep climbs and ancient pathways leading to the historic ruins.
