Kalyngad Fort

Location: Near Satara, Maharashtra

Kalyangad Fort, also known as Nandgiri Fort, is a historically significant hill fort located in the Satara district of Maharashtra. It is situated at an altitude of approximately 3,537 feet (1,078 meters) above sea level and was strategically important in the Maratha Empire. The fort is known for its strong fortifications, deep rock-cut caves, and breathtaking views of the surrounding Sahyadri mountain range.

Kalyangad was originally built by the Shilahara dynasty in the 12th century. It later came under the control of the Bahmani Sultanate and subsequently the Adilshahi dynasty of Bijapur. However, in 1673, Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj captured the fort as part of his expansion of Swarajya, strengthening its defenses and using it as a strategic outpost to monitor enemy movements in the region.

After the death of Shivaji Maharaj, the fort was taken over by the Mughals under Emperor Aurangzeb. However, in 1707, after the fall of the Mughal Empire, the fort was recaptured by Chhatrapati Shahu Maharaj. During the Peshwa era, Kalyangad continued to serve as a key military base and was also used for storing grains, ammunition, and supplies. In 1818, after the defeat of the Marathas in the Third Anglo-Maratha War, the fort was taken over by the British.

One of the most unique features of Kalyangad is its rock-cut caves, believed to have been used by Buddhist monks in ancient times. These caves house a water cistern that remains filled with potable water throughout the year. The fort also has remnants of old structures, including bastions, gateways, and storage rooms.

Today, Kalyangad Fort is a popular trekking and historical destination. The trek to the top is moderately easy, with well-defined pathways and scenic views. Visitors can explore the ruins, admire the stunning landscapes, and experience the rich history of the Maratha era. The fort stands as a symbol of the resilience and strategic acumen of the Marathas.
